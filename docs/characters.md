# Character Philosophy

The characters in **Summer Circuits** are intentionally written as ordinary people.

They are not heroes, villains, or exaggerated archetypes. Each character has strengths, flaws, insecurities, and lives that continue beyond the scenes shown to the player.

Although inspired by real experiences, every character should stand on their own as a fictional individual.

The story emphasizes believable relationships built through shared experiences rather than dramatic conflict.

# Isaac

## Role

Playable Protagonist

### Age

18

### Personality

Quiet.

Observant.

Patient.

Thoughtful.

Isaac rarely speaks unless he has something meaningful to say. He prefers listening, observing people, and understanding how things work. Although others often describe him as intelligent, he simply enjoys learning and solving problems.

His emotions are genuine but usually hidden behind careful thought and quiet actions.

### Strengths

* Technical problem solving
* Calm under pressure
* Reliable
* Patient teacher
* Good listener

### Weaknesses

* Overthinks situations
* Hesitates to express emotions
* Rarely starts conversations
* Underestimates himself

### Interests

* Photography
* Electronics
* Programming
* Learning
* Quiet places

### Speaking Style

Short.

Polite.

Thoughtful.

Becomes noticeably more expressive when discussing technical topics or hobbies.

### Character Arc

Isaac begins the story watching life from the sidelines.

By the end, he learns that some memories are worth being part of rather than simply capturing through a camera lens.

# Cynthia

## Role

Female Lead

### Age

18

### Personality

Warm.

Friendly.

Soft-spoken.

Quietly determined.

Although naturally sociable, Cynthia becomes noticeably shy whenever conversations become personal. She enjoys learning and genuinely appreciates anyone willing to teach her.

One of her favorite ways to connect with people is by comparing them to stories or friends she already knows, making new relationships feel familiar.

### Strengths

* Compassionate
* Curious
* Encouraging
* Optimistic

### Weaknesses

* Easily discouraged
* Doubts herself
* Finds emotional honesty difficult

### Interests

* Electronics
* Spending time with friends
* Learning practical skills

### Speaking Style

Relaxed.

Natural.

Frequently asks questions.

Becomes playful around people she trusts.

### Character Arc

She begins by asking Isaac for help with wiring.

As their friendship grows, she gradually realizes that the qualities she admired in Isaac are the same ones that made her believe he would have gotten along with Jean.

Eventually, she understands that what she first interpreted as compatibility between two people was actually the beginning of her own feelings.

# Ray

## Role

Mentor Figure

### Age

20

### Personality

Patient.

Reflective.

Calm.

Ray enjoys explaining life through electrical concepts, but outside of technical discussions he enjoys exploring different music genres and shares Isaac's dry, dark sense of humor.

Their conversations effortlessly move between electronics, music recommendations, and sarcastic jokes that leave everyone else wondering if they're serious.

Despite his humor, Ray knows exactly when to offer thoughtful advice.

### Strengths

* Mentoring
* Teaching
* Emotional maturity
* Encouraging curiosity

### Weaknesses

* Occasionally overexplains
* Thinks aloud
* Dry humor sometimes surprises others

### Interests

* Electronics
* Music
* Audio equipment
* Teaching

### Speaking Style

Calm.

Thoughtful.

Dryly sarcastic.

Often mixes genuine advice with jokes.

### Character Arc

Ray begins as someone Isaac admires.

By the end, they interact more like equals than mentor and student.

# Jean

## Role

Supporting Character (Off-Screen)

### Age

18

### Personality

Playful.

Observant.

Honest.

Jean never physically appears during the story.

Everything the player learns about her comes through Cynthia's stories and chat messages.

Long before Cynthia develops romantic feelings for Isaac, she often jokes that he reminds her of her close high school friend, Jean.

According to Cynthia, the two share many similarities:

* Quiet personalities
* Dry sense of humor
* Curious minds
* Technical interests
* A tendency to overthink
* Preferring meaningful conversations over large social gatherings

Because of these similarities, Cynthia playfully suggests that Isaac and Jean would probably become close friends if they ever met.

Isaac finds the comparison amusing.

Although he has never met Jean, he gradually imagines her as being almost a female counterpart to himself.

Whether that impression is actually accurate—or simply shaped by Cynthia's storytelling—is intentionally left ambiguous.

### Strengths

* Reads people well
* Gives honest advice
* Loyal friend
* Naturally connects people with similar personalities

### Weaknesses

* Sometimes assumes people will naturally get along
* Can be unexpectedly blunt
* Enjoys teasing her friends

### Interests

*(As described by Cynthia)*

* Technology
* Learning new things
* Quiet hobbies
* Spending time with close friends

### Speaking Style

Never heard directly.

Jean's personality exists entirely through Cynthia's stories.

### Narrative Purpose

Jean expands Cynthia's world beyond the TESDA group.

She acts as an indirect mirror for Isaac, allowing both Cynthia and the player to understand him through comparison rather than direct exposition.

Ironically, Cynthia's repeated comparisons between Isaac and Jean gradually help her realize that the person she has come to admire isn't Jean's "counterpart" at all—it's Isaac himself.

Jean unknowingly becomes the catalyst that helps Cynthia understand her own feelings, despite never appearing on screen.

# Character Writing Rules

To maintain consistent characterization:

* Isaac listens more than he speaks.
* Cynthia naturally relates new people to familiar experiences.
* Jess breaks awkward silence with humor.
* Winston encourages through playful teasing.
* Ray balances thoughtful advice with dry humor.
* Joe teaches through experience rather than speeches.
* Rena quietly keeps everyone organized.
* Jean influences the story without ever appearing on screen.

---

# Final Note

Every character in **Summer Circuits** contributes to a world that feels lived in and genuine.

Rather than driving the plot through dramatic conflict, they shape Isaac's journey through ordinary conversations, shared experiences, and quiet moments.

Together, they remind the player that some of life's most meaningful relationships begin not with extraordinary events, but with simple conversations, small acts of kindness, and the people who unexpectedly bring us together.
