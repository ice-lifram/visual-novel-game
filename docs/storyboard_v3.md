# Story Overview

Summer Circuits follows Isaac, an introverted newcomer attending a month-long TESDA Electrical Installation and Maintenance training during his summer vacation.

What begins as a simple technical training slowly becomes one of the most memorable summers of his life as he discovers friendship, belonging, and eventually love.

The story remains linear throughout the entire experience until the final scene, where the player's decision determines Isaac's response to Cynthia's confession.

Estimated playtime is **10–20 minutes**.

---

## Summary

Isaac arrives at the TESDA Electrical Installation and Maintenance training with little expectation beyond learning practical electrical skills.

The classroom already feels lively.

Everyone seems to know someone.

Isaac quietly takes a seat.

As introductions begin, the class is asked to elect officers.

Although uncomfortable with the responsibility, he accepts the role.

During the introductions, the player briefly meets:

## Objective

Create Isaac and Cynthia's first meaningful interaction.

## Summary

Students begin assembling their first electrical wiring activity.

Isaac quickly finishes his plate.

Nearby, Cynthia quietly struggles with her own connections.

After hesitating, she asks:

> "Can you check if I wired this correctly?"

Rather than fixing it for her, Isaac explains what went wrong and allows her to solve it herself.

Jess notices the interaction and jokingly remarks:

> "Looks like you found yourself a personal tutor."

The three laugh.

The atmosphere becomes noticeably lighter.

# Narrative Philosophy

Summer Circuits tells a story through ordinary moments rather than dramatic events.

Every scene exists to move the relationship forward.

The player witnesses two strangers gradually become friends, then best friends, before finally deciding whether that friendship becomes something more.

The story's emotional strength comes not from grand gestures, but from the quiet moments that slowly change two people's lives.

## Revision Notes (v2 Planning)

### Updated Chapter 3 — Inner Conflicts

**New Supporting Character**
- Michael (Isaac's senior high school friend and former EIM classmate)

**Revised Narrative Purpose**
Instead of remaining entirely inside Isaac's thoughts, the chapter contrasts Isaac's loneliness in the new environment with the familiarity of an old friendship.

While watching Cynthia and Jess comfortably interact with other trainees, Isaac begins doubting whether he truly belongs. To distract himself, he calls Michael and gives him a quick tour of the training laboratory. Their casual conversation about electrical equipment, old classmates, and memories briefly lifts Isaac's mood before he returns to class.

The chapter ends with Isaac realizing that although his old life remains important, he also needs to give himself a chance to build new connections.


### Training Class Dynamics (Updated)

- Every trainee is attending Sir Lex's EIM training for the first time.
- No trainee personally knows Sir Lex before the program begins.
- Differences between trainees come from their educational and life backgrounds—not prior familiarity with the training.
- Winston and Ray already know each other because they are classmates and close friends in their third year of Electrical Engineering, providing one established friendship within the otherwise unfamiliar class.
