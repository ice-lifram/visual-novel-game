# Summer Circuits

## Characters

**Version:** 2.0
**Status:** Story Bible

---

# Character Philosophy

The characters in **Summer Circuits** are intentionally written as ordinary people.

They are not heroes, villains, or exaggerated archetypes. Each character has strengths, flaws, insecurities, and lives that continue beyond the scenes shown to the player.

Although inspired by real experiences, every character stands on their own as a fictional individual.

**Summer Circuits is ultimately a story about ordinary people gradually becoming important to one another through everyday experiences. The romance is not driven by grand gestures, but by trust, shared moments, and personal growth.**

---

# Isaac

## Role
Playable Protagonist

### Age
18

### Personality
- Quiet
- Observant
- Curious
- Humble
- Responsible
- Overthinker

Isaac rarely speaks unless he feels he has something meaningful to contribute. While others often see him as calm and capable, his inner thoughts reveal someone who constantly questions himself, second-guesses conversations, and quietly worries about disappointing those around him.

Despite this, he genuinely enjoys helping others and becomes noticeably animated whenever discussions involve engineering, electronics, or photography.

### Strengths
- Technical problem solving
- Calm under pressure
- Reliable
- Patient teacher
- Good listener

### Weaknesses
- Overthinks situations
- Hesitates to express emotions
- Rarely starts conversations
- Underestimates himself
- Assumes too much before asking
- Finds it difficult to believe people genuinely appreciate him

### Interests
- Photography
- Electronics
- Programming
- Learning
- Quiet places

### Character Arc
Isaac begins the story believing he is simply passing through another chapter of his life.

Over time, he learns that meaningful relationships are not distractions from his goals—they become part of the journey itself.

By the end, he finds the courage to express his feelings and realizes he deserves to be loved despite the doubts he carries.

### First Impression of Cynthia
- Finally someone his age.
- Probably already has her own friend group.
- Seems low-key.
- Someone he might become friends with.

# Cynthia

## Role
Female Lead

### Age
18

### Personality
- Gentle
- Quiet
- Soft-spoken
- Thoughtful
- Curious

Although Cynthia initially struggles to speak confidently around unfamiliar people, she becomes much more expressive once she feels comfortable. She enjoys learning through other people and naturally connects new experiences to familiar ones.

### Strengths
- Compassionate
- Curious
- Encouraging
- Optimistic

### Weaknesses
- Easily discouraged
- Doubts herself
- Finds emotional honesty difficult

### Character Arc
She begins by asking Isaac for help with wiring schematics.

Their friendship slowly grows through shared experiences until she realizes that the qualities she admired in Isaac were never because he reminded her of someone else—they were uniquely his.

### First Impression of Isaac
- Thought he was already a college student.
- Impressed by his technical background.
- Quiet rather than unfriendly.
- Thoughtful.
- Good listener.
- Initially believed they were very different people.
- Later realized they were surprisingly alike.

---

# Jess

## Role
Cynthia's Best Friend

### Personality
Energetic, outgoing and observant.

Jess dislikes awkward silence. Whenever everyone hesitates, she is usually the first person willing to say or do something. She enjoys teasing friends but is also surprisingly perceptive.

---

# Winston

## Role
Older Classmate

Supportive and approachable. Acts like an older brother to the younger trainees and helps everyone feel included.

---

# Ray

## Role
Mentor Figure

Patient, reflective and calm.

Ray has a habit of turning ordinary conversations into life lessons using engineering analogies. His dry humor often catches people off guard.

---

# Joe

## Role
Senior Mentor

Quiet, dependable and experienced.

Joe earns respect not by demanding it, but through quiet competence.

---

# Rena

## Role
Class Secretary

Organized, responsible and friendly.

Although she rarely becomes the center of attention, many important moments happen because of her quiet initiative.

---

# Jean

## Role
Supporting Character (Off-Screen)

Jean never physically appears.

She exists entirely through Cynthia's stories and messages.

Jean unknowingly becomes a measuring stick for Cynthia. The more Cynthia compares Isaac to Jean, the more she notices the qualities that are uniquely Isaac's.

---

# Eunoe

## Role
Isaac's Former Admiration

### Narrative Purpose

Eunoe represents Isaac's old belief that some people are simply beyond his reach.

She is not a love triangle nor a rival.

Instead, she explains why Isaac enters the story believing that pursuing love is not worth the emotional risk.

---

# Character Writing Rules

- Isaac listens more than he speaks.
- Isaac's internal thoughts often contradict his outward confidence.
- Cynthia gradually becomes more expressive as trust develops.
- Jess breaks awkward silence.
- Winston encourages through playful teasing.
- Ray teaches using engineering analogies.
- Joe teaches through actions rather than speeches.
- Rena quietly keeps everyone organized.
- Jean influences the story without appearing.
- Characters are allowed to misunderstand one another. First impressions should evolve naturally through shared experiences.

---

# Final Note

Every character contributes to a world that feels lived in rather than dramatic.

The heart of Summer Circuits is watching ordinary people slowly replace assumptions with understanding, and acquaintances with genuine connection.
